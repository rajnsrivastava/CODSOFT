VARIETIES

Lakshmanbhog Mangoes

Exclusively grown in the Malda district of West Bengal, Lakshmanbhog mangoes are usually available in the months of June and July.

Gulab Khaas Mangoes

Gulab Khaas Mango is a delectable fruit that exhibits rosy flavour and tastes. Available between May and June, these mangoes are sold in abundance in states such as Bihar, West Bengal, and Jharkhand.

Alphonso Mangoes

Available during mid-July, Alphonso Mangoes are internationally loved for their sunshine yellow appearance and delightful taste.

Amrapali Mangoes

Amrapali Mangoes are a hybrid variety created in 1971. A cross breed between Dasheri and Neelam Mangoes, Amrapali is grown in farms and orchards across India and is known for its deep red flesh once fully ripe.

Kilichundan Mango

Kilichundan Mangoes are relatively large in size and are sold in large quantities in various regions of Kerala. The name of this fruit has derived from the fact that its end resembles the beak of the bird and this is exactly what Kilichundan means in the Malayalam language.

Safeda Mangoes

Safeda or Banganpali or Benishan Mango is a popular fruit in several regions in Andhra Pradesh; especially in Banganpalle town, which is evident from its name. Often called as The King of Mangoes in South India.
